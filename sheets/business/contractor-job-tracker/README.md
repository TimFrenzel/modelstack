# Contractor Job Tracker

**Category:** Business

**Summary:** A job-level profitability tracker for a small general contractor (three guys, 8-12 active jobs). The owner got burned when material costs and scope changes ate into margins, so he built this to see which jobs actually made money. It's simple on purpose. The second sheet is barely formatted because he only opens it when rates change.

**Structure:** `Job Log` has a summary box at the top showing total jobs, completed jobs, average margin on completed work, best and worst job by margin, and total profit. The main table below tracks 12 jobs with job number, client, description, status (done, active, or on hold), quoted price, materials, labor hours, labor cost (formula pulling the shop rate from Sheet 2), subcontractor costs, other costs, total actual, profit, margin percent, and notes. Seven jobs are completed, three are active with partial costs, two are on hold with only the quote filled in. `Rates & Assumptions` is intentionally bare: shop rate at $45/hr, target margin, insurance, truck payment, and a few scratch notes.
