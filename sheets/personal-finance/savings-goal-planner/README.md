# Savings Goal Planner

**Category:** Personal Finance

**Summary:** I like this one for people who know they need an emergency fund, but also have five other things they’re trying to save for at the same time. The emergency fund target comes from actual monthly essentials, which makes it feel grounded instead of arbitrary. After that, the workbook shows how the other goals move at the current pace and under a boosted scenario.

**Structure:** `Monthly Expenses` holds 12 months of essential spending by category and calculates the average monthly burn. `Emergency Fund` turns that into a target amount from the coverage month input, then shows shortfall, projected funded date, and the boosted contribution case. `Goals` tracks target amount, current balance, monthly contribution, priority, projected months, and the faster path if the emergency fund contribution gets redirected later. `Contributions` logs actual monthly deposits against planned totals. `Projections` runs 24 months forward for the baseline and boosted paths. `Dashboard` keeps the emergency fund current versus target chart, goal progress chart, and the summary stats together.
