# Fifty Thirty Twenty

**Category:** Personal Finance

**Summary:** I'd pull this up when I want the 50 30 20 rule to be more than a rough idea. It classifies every category into needs, wants, or savings, then checks actual spending against net income month by month. The extra paycheck planner is useful too, especially if bills land unevenly across the month.

**Structure:** `Income` walks from gross pay through deductions to monthly net income and then sets the 50 percent, 30 percent, and 20 percent dollar targets. `Category Map` is the classification table, and any bucket change there flows through the rest of the workbook. `Expense Log` stores the transaction level data with category and recurring dropdowns. `Monthly Tracker` groups actuals and targets for each bucket by month and adds the delta plus the actual percent of net income. `Paycheck Planner` splits recurring bills across two paychecks. `Analysis` finishes with the annual split pie, the monthly adherence lines, and the stacked monthly bucket chart.
